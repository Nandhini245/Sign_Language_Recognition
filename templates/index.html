What is Sign Language Recognition and its imporatant?
Sign language recognition (SLR) is a technology that enables computers to interpret and understand sign languages used by the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. It combines computer vision and machine learning to analyze gestures, hand movements, facial expressions to accurately translate them into spoken or written language.
  • Bridging the Communication Gap:It allows seamless communication between people with hearing impairments and the rest of society.
  • Increased Accessibility:Enhances accessibility in various sectors like education, healthcare, and customer service.
  • Real-Time Assistance:Offers real-time translation for daily conversations, making communication faster and more effective.

    Implementation of project

    Sign language recognition systems process video or image data to identify patterns in hand gestures, facial expressions, and body movement. The steps typically include:

    • Data Collection: Videos or images of sign language gestures are collected.
    • Feature Extraction: Hand, face, and pose data are analyzed using computer vision algorithms.
    • Pattern Recognition: Machine learning models identify and classify gestures.
    • Prediction: The system predicts the corresponding meaning in text or speech form.
